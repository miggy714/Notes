\section{Section 4A - Gibbs and Auxillary functions}
Gibbs: $$dG = dH - Tds$$
\subsection{Gibbs derivation}
All systems tend toward a lower "Energy state". This process acquires entropy (maximizes configuration).
\\
Consider the three laws applied to a reversible process:
\begin{itemize}
    \item 1st law: 
    \\$dU = \delta q - \delta w$ 
    \\ $dU = \delta q_{rev} - PdV$ (I)
    \item 2nd law: 
    \\$dS \ge \frac{\delta q}{T}$
    \\$dS = \frac{\delta q_{rev}}{T}$
    \\$\delta q_{rev} = T ds$ (II)
    \item 3rd law:
    \\$S = 0$ at 0K
    \item Enthalpy:
    \\ $H \equiv U + PV$
    \\$dH = dU + PdV + vdP$ (III)
\end{itemize}
\subsection{cont.}
(II) $\circ$ (I): $dU = Tds - PdV$ (IV)
\\
(IV) $\circ$ (III): $dH = (Tds - PdV) + PdV + VdP$
\\$dH = TdS + VdP$ (IV)

\subsection{For a process at constant T and P}
(IV)$\Big|_{P,T}$: $dH = Tds$
\\
\begin{itemize}
    \item This equation is a criteria for sponteneity
    \item It displays an equilibrium which can be used to predict the direction of a reaction
    \item This equation balances both:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item enthalpy (naturally lower energy state is preferred)
        \item entropy (naturally tends towards a statistically favored state)
    \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}
\subsection{Gibbs Free Energy}
$$G \equiv H - TS$$
$$ \Delta G = \Delta H - T \Delta S$$
$$ dG = dU + VdP + PdV - S dT - T dS $$
$$dG = \delta q - \delta w + VdP + PdV - S dT - T dS$$
$$dG = TdS - PdV + VdP + PdV - SdT - TdS$$
$$dG = VdP - SdT$$
\\
The final line indicates that the equilibrium criteria varies with 
temperature and pressure
